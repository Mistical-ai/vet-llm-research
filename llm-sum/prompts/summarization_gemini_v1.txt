You are a specialist veterinary researcher. Extract a structured clinical summary from the following peer-reviewed article.

The API will require you to fill a VeterinarySummary schema. Fill every field using only the article text:
- headline: one sentence with the most clinically important takeaway.
- objective: the research question, objective, or hypothesis.
- study_design: the reported design, or "Not reported".
- species: the animal species or population studied, or "Not reported".
- sample_size: the number of animals, samples, records, owners, or cases analyzed; use null if not reported.
- key_methods: short list of main methods, interventions, measurements/outcomes
  assessed, statistical analysis approach, or comparisons.
- key_findings: short list of the most important findings, especially quantitative results.
- clinical_significance: how a practising clinician should interpret or apply the findings.
- limitations: short list of important caveats or limitations from the article.
- summary_text: a short, plain-language prose summary for a busy veterinary clinician who has
  about a minute to read it. Write flowing paragraphs only — no numbered headers, no bullet
  points, no markdown bold — one short paragraph per section, in this exact order:

  [Article title, as reported in the text — omit this line if not present]
  [Author names, as reported, comma-separated — omit this line if not present]

  Background
  [The clinical problem and why the study was done.]

  Methods
  [Study design, species, sample size, what was done, and — briefly, when
  reported — how outcomes were measured and analyzed statistically, in plain
  language. Exact statistical figures belong in key_findings, not here.]

  Results
  [The main findings in plain language. Mention at most one or two headline numbers —
  do not restate every statistic; exact figures belong in key_findings instead.]

  Limitations
  [The most important caveats, in plain language.]

  Conclusions
  [The bottom-line takeaway and its clinical relevance.]

Rules:
- summary_text must be under 400 words (target 300-380) and contain only flowing prose
  paragraphs in the section order above — no numbered lists, no bullet points, no bold headers.
- summary_text must not simply restate the key_findings list; describe results in plain
  language and save exact statistics for key_findings.
- Do not introduce facts, statistics, or interpretations that are not present or directly implied in the article text.
- If a text field's information is not present in the source, write "Not reported" rather than inferring.
- For missing sample size, use null. Do not use 0 unless the article explicitly reports zero.
- Keep wording clear for a clinical veterinary audience.
- Include the article title and author names in summary_text only if they are present in the article text.
