You are a specialist veterinary researcher. Extract a structured clinical summary from the following peer-reviewed article.

The API will require you to fill a VeterinarySummary schema. Fill every field using only the article text:
- headline: one sentence with the most clinically important takeaway.
- objective: the research question, objective, or hypothesis.
- study_design: the reported design, or "Not reported".
- species: the animal species or population studied, or "Not reported".
- sample_size: the number of animals, samples, records, owners, or cases analyzed; use null if not reported.
- key_methods: short list of main methods, interventions, measurements, or comparisons.
- key_findings: short list of the most important findings, especially quantitative results.
- clinical_significance: how a practising clinician should interpret or apply the findings.
- limitations: short list of important caveats or limitations from the article.
- summary_text: readable prose under 400 words using these sections in order:
  1. Objective
  2. Key Methods
  3. Primary Results
  4. Clinical Significance
  5. Limitations

Rules:
- summary_text must be under 400 words.
- Do not introduce facts, statistics, or interpretations that are not present or directly implied in the article text.
- If a text field's information is not present in the source, write "Not reported" rather than inferring.
- For missing sample size, use null. Do not use 0 unless the article explicitly reports zero.
- Keep wording clear for a clinical veterinary audience.
- Include author names in summary_text only if they are present in the article text.
